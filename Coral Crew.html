Deep Sea(quencing) Anemone Mitogenomes: Understanding Evolutionary Relationships

Using Biopython and Phylip, we assembled a phylogeny — a family tree — of nine anemones. These deep-sea dwellers are very difficult to study, as they can live thousands of meters underwater. We recently received sequence data from a handful of new species and have assembled and analyzed these genomes in the greater context of the cnidarian family tree. In particular, we examined the placement of a peculiar organism, Relicanthus, within our anemone group, and found that it is an outlier among known anemone species.

Languages, libraries, and packages we used:

  • The programming language we used for this project was Python.
  • The libraries and packages we used were Biopython and Phylip.

Some challenges we encountered:

  • Something challenging was creating new functions. They had to make sure they understood the concept before being able to make the function.
  • Understanding the science.
  • Another challenge was distance matrix, they had to take two sequences and used the Hammon distance. Had to find the distance in percentages in two sequences.
